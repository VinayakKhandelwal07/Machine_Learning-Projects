Predict Your Insurance Premiums

Description:

Overview: This project builds a machine learning model to predict insurance premiums based on individual characteristics. By analyzing age, gender, smoking status, region, and number of children, the model provides accurate insurance price estimates.

Objective: Create a predictive model to assist insurers in setting fair rates and help customers plan their insurance budgets.

Inputs:

  • Age: Age of the insured.
  • Gender: Gender of the insured.
  • Smoker Status: Whether the insured is a smoker (Yes/No).
  • Region: Geographical location of the insured.
  • Number of Children: Count of children in the insured’s household.
Modeling Techniques:
  • Linear Regression: Models the linear relationship between inputs and insurance charges.
  • Decision Tree Regression: Captures non-linear patterns and interactions among features.
  • Random Forest Regression: Improves accuracy by averaging multiple decision trees.
Implementation:
  • Data Collection: Historical data on insurance premiums and features.
  • Preprocessing: Data cleaning and feature selection.
  • Training and Evaluation: Models trained and evaluated using metrics like MAE and RMSE.
Applications:
  • Insurance Companies: For setting accurate premiums.
  • Customers: To estimate and plan for insurance costs.
  • Regulatory Bodies: For ensuring transparent pricing.
Conclusion: The model effectively uses linear, decision tree, and random forest regressions to predict insurance costs, supporting better decision-making for both insurers and customers.
